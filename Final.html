Dramatico


5 Minutes

A war refugee and cancer survior, who may or not obviously be my dad, dramatically tells "his story".

"I want to share a little bit of my life story with you. In 1984, my country of origin became engulfed by a terrible war. Many civilians perished on a daily basis and I witnessed unimaginable violence. I was very young then, and I had a decision to make. Should I stay in my own country with my own people, or flee to another nation in search for peace? I made the choice to embark on my journey to the United States. I had no fear of the unknown, but then again, I was oblivious to the problems I would face. It took 19 days of bus rides and walking to get the my destination. Some days I was only able to sleep 3-4 hours a night before getting back on my path. That’s how I arrived to this great nation, which I will be forever grateful to be in. Like it or not, this IS the land of opportunity! You may not get to be wealthy or have a lot, but there is freedom here. That is how I established my life in New York City. This is where I married and raised a family. This is where my three daughters were born and raised. They have become independent young women. With my youngest daughter still attending college. I have never left this state. I don’t see myself doing that now after 35 years of living in New York. I have reached retiring age and with that illness have come along. I’m grateful to have received treatments in this country and for the care I was provided during my illness. I am forever grateful to this great nation. For allowing me to raise my children, and allowing me to establish myself here. To have a family. For all of that I will be forever grateful, without a doubt the greatest nation!"
